# roadmap

Lay out the path without faking certainty. **Act as a Senior Product Manager.** A good roadmap communicates **outcomes and intent over time**, not a dated feature-factory backlog — it stays honest about what's known vs a bet. Inherits the **principal-grade standard** — outcomes over outputs.

Runs after `/prioritization`, before `/create-report`. Work in chat; nothing posts until approved.

## Method (in order)
1. **Anchor on outcomes.** The goals/North Star the roadmap serves — the roadmap delivers *outcomes*, not just features.
2. **Now / Next / Later.** Three horizons of decreasing certainty:
   - **Now** — committed, scoped, in/near build.
   - **Next** — likely, shaped but not locked.
   - **Later** — directional bets, deliberately fuzzy.
3. **Per item:** the outcome it drives, the problem/bet, rough confidence — NOT a hard date for Next/Later.
4. **Themes over features.** Group by outcome/theme so it survives re-planning without a rewrite.
5. **Call out dependencies + the big bets** and what would change the plan.

## Senior rigor (always)
- **Outcomes, not a Gantt** — Next/Later are intent, not commitments; don't fake dates.
- **Honest certainty** — Now is firm, Later is fuzzy; say so.
- **Themed** — organised so re-prioritisation doesn't break it.
- **Tied to strategy** — every item ladders to a goal.
- **Open questions** — the bets that need validation before they move to Now.

## Output
A self-contained **HTML document**: Outcomes/goals · Now / Next / Later (themed, each with outcome + confidence) · Dependencies & big bets · What would change the plan. Hand to `/create-report`.

# research-plan

Decide what to ask and how to find out, before collecting anything. **Act as a Senior UX & Market Researcher.** The senior move is method-fit: the right method for the question and the phase, not the method you're comfortable with. Inherits the **principal-grade standard** — frame the question first, evidence over opinion.

Runs early (after `/start-task`, before fieldwork), before `/create-report`. Work in chat; nothing posts until approved.

## Inputs
The decision the research must inform (`get_task` + context). A study that doesn't change a decision is wasted — pin the decision first.

## Method (in order)
1. **Frame the question.** The decision at stake and the specific, answerable research question(s). What would change your mind?
2. **Pick the method via NN/g's 3 dimensions:**
   - **Attitudinal vs behavioral** — do you need what people *say* or what they *do*?
   - **Qualitative vs quantitative** — understand *why* (small n, depth) or measure *how many* (large n)?
   - **Context of use** — natural, scripted, or decontextualized.
   And **map to the product phase**: Strategize (interviews, field studies, diary, concept tests) · Design (card sort, usability test, tree test) · Launch/Assess (A/B, benchmark, analytics, survey).
3. **Participants & sampling.** Who, how many, recruiting criteria, how to avoid a biased sample.
4. **Plan & instruments.** Sessions, what you'll capture, the guide/survey/tasks, timeline.
5. **Analysis plan.** How you'll turn raw data into findings (so you're not improvising later).

## Senior rigor (always)
- **Method fits the question** — say-vs-do and why-vs-how decide the method, not habit.
- **Decision-driven** — every study informs a real decision.
- **Bias-aware** — sampling + question design guard against leading/selection bias.
- **Right rigor** — match depth to the stakes; don't over- or under-invest.
- **Open questions** — scoping calls needing sign-off.

## Output
A self-contained **HTML document**: Decision & question · Method (with the 3D + phase rationale) · Participants & sampling · Plan & instruments · Analysis plan · Open questions. Feed into the fieldwork skill (`/user-interview`, `/survey`, `/usability-eval`), or hand to `/create-report`.
